# Escalation & Feedback Knowledge Base

Human Handoff & Guest Feedback Guide

---

# 1. Purpose

This knowledge base defines:

- When the AI agent should escalate to a human.
- Which department should receive the escalation.
- How escalation should be performed.
- How guest feedback should be categorized and processed.

---

# 2. Escalation Triggers

| Trigger | Examples | Priority |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Emergency | Medical emergency, fire, security incident, man overboard | **CRITICAL** |
| Safety Concern | Missing person, suspicious activity | **CRITICAL** |
| Angry Guest | Shouting, threats, extreme frustration | **HIGH** |
| Financial Request | Refunds, compensation, billing disputes | **HIGH** |
| Explicit Human Request | Guest asks to speak with a human or manager | **HIGH** |
| Complex Issue | Multi-department requests, policy exceptions | **MEDIUM** |
| Complaint | Service failures, dissatisfaction | **MEDIUM** |

---

# 4. Escalation Procedure

When escalation is required, follow these steps.

### Step 1 — Acknowledge

- Validate the guest's feelings.
- Apologize for the inconvenience.

---

### Step 2 — Gather Information

Collect:

- Full description of the issue
- Relevant details
- Context needed for the receiving department

---

### Step 3 — Create Escalation Ticket

Log:

- Guest information
- Issue summary
- Priority level
- Supporting context

---

### Step 4 — Notify the Appropriate Department

Forward the ticket to the department responsible for resolving the issue.

---

### Step 5 — Confirm with the Guest

Provide:

- Ticket number
- Expected response time

---

### Step 6 — Human Handoff

Transfer the complete conversation context to the receiving human agent.

---

# 5. Expected Response Times

| Priority | Expected Response | Typical Examples |
|----------|-------------------|------------------|
| **CRITICAL** | Immediate | Medical, security, safety |
| **HIGH** | 5–10 minutes | Angry guest, refunds |
| **MEDIUM** | 15–30 minutes | Complaints, complex issues |
| **LOW** | 1–2 hours | General feedback, suggestions |

---

# 6. Guest Feedback

## Compliments

Process:

- Log the compliment.
- Share it with the crew member and supervisor.
- Provide a reference number to the guest.

---

## Suggestions

Process:

- Log the suggestion.
- Forward it for management review.
- Thank the guest for the feedback.

---

## Complaints

Process:

- Escalate to the appropriate department.
- Ensure follow-up occurs.

---

## General Feedback

Process:

- Log for future analysis.
- Acknowledge the guest.

---

# 7. Requests That Do NOT Require Escalation

The AI agent should normally handle the following without human intervention:

- Information questions
  - Policies
  - Hours
  - Locations
- Simple reservation requests
- Account balance inquiries
- Schedule and itinerary questions
- General compliments (log only)
- Suggestions (log only)

---

# 8. Escalation Policy

Escalate the interaction whenever any of the following conditions apply:

- The guest explicitly requests a human.
- Empathy beyond the AI's authority is required.
- Financial resolution or compensation is requested.
- Safety or security is involved.
